Find Me a Rich Man:
The Pier

A design solution based on the problem definition framework of young women searching for young rich bachelors in Sydney, based off the data analysis of the 2016 census.

About

Through observing the 2016 data, it is possible to find the most eligible (and wealthiest) boyfriend - utilising QGIS maps to exactly pin point where the largest number of men aged 25-34 live and exactly how much money they make weekly.

Process

Joining the data set to a map of NSW and setting the variable to what we wish to find - in this case, rich boys.

More Data

Through analysing further data such as marrital status and highest level of education, we are able to find the most eligible bachelor.

Outcome

With all of this important information, a layered map will be made - pin pointing the areas in Sydney where rich, smart and single men can be found.

Final Goal

What can be done with this information? The world is now your oyster - go and find your man.

High Weekly Income


Through the observation of the 2016 data set showing the weekly income of males between the ages of 25 and 35 in Sydney, Mesh blocks are colored accordingly to show income levels. The lighter blue areas have lower weekly income and the darker purple areas are the highest income areas. Areas in the Eastern Suburbs as well as in the City have higher weekly income rates, as well as in industrial areas due to a higher population of men and higher paying jobs. Expensive areas in the North Shore and around beaches are mostly light purple or blue not because of a lower income but because they are largely populated by families and retireees.

The Latte line runs down through the middle of Northern Sydney and through to the Eastern suburbs which doesn't follow a specific pattern but does enclose the wealthier areas of Sydney and the main universities and industrial workplaces which makes sense as they would be heavily populated with young men.

Through the observation and comparison of data sets, it is evident that the welathiest, most educated single men are situated in the Eastern suburbs (Bondi Area), Northern beaches and inner city. With this information, you are now able to find the most eligible bachelor.

Problem

The issue that arises from this data is that the most eligible bachelors are localised within their respective areas, making it a challenge to potentially meet one as they aren't in one central location. Due to their wealth, this also means they live in extremely expensive areas which are simply unattainable for regular girls like myself.

What Needs to be Addressed?

The main problems that must be identified include the location of the men: the wealthiest areas are too spread out to be able to pinpoint where you could find your bachelor, and the fact that they may not go for the regular girl: most girls can't afford to live in those areas so how will they get noticed by the wealthy men?

Who is Involved?

The main focus is on the rich men and young women of any kind who are looking for a partner.

Solution

Taking all of the previous imformation into account, there are a number of possible solutions; However, the final solution is big, exciting and addresses all of the issues listed above.
The Pier

What is The Pier?

The Pier is a new and exciting estate-like establishment that spans the harbour, linking Point Piper in the city to Ashfield Park in the North Shore. It acts as accomodation and a safe haven for young women and boasts beautiful views of the harbour, a native park and the City of Sydney. Whilst it accomodates young women, it's facilities are open to the public - with cafes and state of the art public meeting places. It hosts beach parties and celebratory events aimed at both men and women in their 20's.

How Does it Address These Issues?

The Pier links two of the wealthiest areas together - The City and The North Shore, making it easily accessible and a hot spot for wealthy men living in the area. The parties and events as well as the facilities in the area are an excellent way to attract young people and encourage them to meet and get to know one-another. It also allows young women to live in an exciting new area without the excessive price tag as it is exclusively for women only. This also adds an extra element of safety to the solution. Whilst the plan is intended in a light-hearted and fun way to get to know new people, there are always elements of risk - this is intended to minimise all risk factors.

The Design


This is an interactive draft design of the Pier and all of the buildings it is made up of. It spans across the harbour and is designed in a similar style to Tetris - with each building fitting into a uniform structure whilst maintaining individuality, representing the unique nature of human kind whilst giving off a fun and playful atmosphere.






























Cafe Design


This design is one of the many public meeting spaces, it is open and inviting with clean lines and minimalistic colors - making it modern yet fun with the contoured glass roof. Because it is located right on the water, the contours are used to imitate the sea and the space is not fully enclosed all year round to create an open and atmospheric feel.
